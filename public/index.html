Supply & holder charts

Top wallets vs circulating supply

Each slice is a share of circulating supply held by the top 10, top 11–100, optional extended cohort (when limit ≥ 250), and all remaining wallets.

Labeled vs unlabeled supply

Vybe-labeled wallets versus anonymous addresses within the fetched top holders, plus supply held outside that group.

Holders by % of supply (fetched rows)

Count of wallets in each per-holder % band among the rows returned for the current limit and page (not the full chain).

Supply concentration (fetched cohort)

Cumulative % of circulating supply held by the top k rows (by current sort). Gini uses each holder’s % share plus a synthetic “remainder” bucket so totals reach 100%.

Lorenz curve (supply inequality)

Cumulative supply share (y) vs share of ranked holders (x), smallest to largest slice. The dashed line is perfect equality.

Holders by USD position size

Count of fetched rows by wallet USD value (Vybe mark).

Holders by balance (vs median)

Log-style bands relative to the median balance among fetched rows (helps tiny and whale tokens on one chart).

Whale tiers (per-holder % of supply)

Supply share aggregated by holder tier within the fetched top cohort, plus supply outside that cohort.

Labeled groups by combined supply

Each wallet’s Vybe name or first label is split on whitespace; wallets sharing the same first token are summed (% supply and USD). Every group is listed, including a single wallet.

Top 10 wallets by % supply

Largest holders in the current response (respects sort order when viewing other metrics).
